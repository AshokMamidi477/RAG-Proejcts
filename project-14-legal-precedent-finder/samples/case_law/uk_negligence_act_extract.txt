STATUTE EXTRACT — RELEVANT PROVISIONS

Limitation Act 1980 (UK) — Key Provisions for Negligence Claims:

Section 2 — Time limit for actions founded on tort:
An action founded on tort shall not be brought after the expiration of six years from the date
on which the cause of action accrued.

Section 11 — Special time limit for negligence actions in respect of personal injuries:
In the case of actions for damages for negligence, nuisance or breach of duty where the damages
claimed consist of or include damages in respect of personal injuries, the limitation period is
three years from: (a) the date on which the cause of action accrued; or (b) the date of the
claimant's knowledge (if later).

Section 14 — Definition of date of knowledge:
References to a person's date of knowledge are references to the date on which he first had
knowledge of: (a) that the injury was significant; (b) that the injury was attributable to the
act or omission alleged to constitute negligence; (c) the identity of the defendant.

Civil Liability (Contribution) Act 1978:
Section 1: Any person liable in respect of any damage suffered by another person may recover
contribution from any other person liable in respect of the same damage.

Section 2: The amount of contribution recoverable shall be such as may be found by the court
to be just and equitable having regard to the extent of that person's responsibility for the damage.
