CLINICAL TRIAL SUMMARY — TRIAL ID: CT-2022-CV-014

Title: SGLT2 Inhibitor for Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction

Phase: Phase III
Disease Area: Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF)
Sponsor: CardioTrial Foundation
Duration: 36 months
Sample Size: 1,124 participants

PRIMARY COMPOSITE ENDPOINT:
Cardiovascular death OR worsening heart failure (hospitalisation or urgent visit).

RESULTS:
Primary endpoint occurred in 21.2% of SGLT2 inhibitor arm vs 26.7% of placebo arm.
Hazard ratio: 0.78 (95% CI 0.64–0.95, p=0.011).
Number needed to treat: 18 over 36 months.

SECONDARY ENDPOINTS:
Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire score improved by 4.3 points vs 1.8 (p=0.002).
eGFR decline was slower in treatment arm (-1.4 vs -2.7 ml/min/1.73m2/year).

SAFETY:
Genital mycotic infections higher in treatment arm (9% vs 2%).
No significant difference in DKA events. Amputation risk comparable between arms.

CONCLUSION:
SGLT2 inhibition significantly reduced cardiovascular events in HFpEF patients and improved
quality of life, representing a new treatment option for this historically difficult-to-treat population.
